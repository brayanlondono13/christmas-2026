Four nights, three full days. Each day has a rhythm, but nothing is required. The house is the centerpiece.

We fly to Denver and caravan through the mountains to Breckenridge. By evening, we're settled into our mountain mansion.

Before You Go

What to Pack

Winter Layers

Jacket, gloves, hat, scarf, snow boots. Base layers and fleece for dry cold at altitude.

Ski Gear (optional)

Bring your own or rent in Breckenridge. Rental packages start around $50/day.

Sunglasses & Sunscreen

High altitude means strong UV, even in winter. The sun reflects off the snow.

Swimsuit

For the hot tub. You'll use it every night.

Games & Entertainment

Board games, card games, puzzles for cabin nights. The game room has some, but bring favorites.

White Elephant Gift

Set a $20–25 limit. We'll do the exchange Saturday night at the cabin.

Sleds

For Carter Park. Buy cheap ones at Walmart in Frisco on the way in, or bring from home.

Water Bottle

Hydration is critical at altitude. Bring a reusable bottle and drink constantly.

Altitude Advisory

Breckenridge sits at 9,600 feet, nearly two miles above sea level. You may feel lightheaded, short of breath, or get headaches the first day. Drink plenty of water, take it easy on day one, go light on alcohol that first night, and give your body time to adjust. It gets much better by day two.
